First, what is an Eating Saboteur?

Think of yourself as a system made up of parts. One part of you genuinely wants to reach your goals. Another part reaches for your go-to foods in the moment. When you've struggled with eating for years, it's usually because an Eating Saboteur part has quietly taken charge.

When a saboteur is running things, it looks like:

  • Grazing or snacking when you're not hungry
  • Night-time, stress, emotional or reward eating
  • Once you start snacking, you can't stop
  • Eating in a way that feels out of control

The part most women miss

Here's what matters: your saboteur's intention is positive. It's trying to protect you, comfort you or give you something back. It's the strategy that costs you, leading to weight regain and wearing away at how you feel about yourself.

And because saboteurs are subconscious, you don't notice them in the moment. You just find yourself snacking. That's why willpower keeps failing. You can't out-think a part of you that operates below your thinking. The work is meeting these over-protective parts and updating the job they do. It starts with knowing which one is loudest for you.
